Introduction

About This Document

People Centric Leadership (PCL) exists because too many organisations treat values as a tick-box exercise, too many leaders operate without credible evidence, and too many voices go unheard. This brand was founded on the conviction that when we understand what people truly value—and how they perceive each other's values—we unlock the conditions for better leadership, stronger teams, and a more harmonious society.

These guidelines capture our strategic identity. Every decision—from colour to copy, from typography to tone—is rooted in five personality traits that define this brand: grounded, soulful, precise, quietly courageous, and purposefully warm.

"This is not a rigid rulebook. PCL is a brand built on openness and human connection. Use these guidelines as a compass, not a cage."

PCL Brand Identity

Brand Personality

PCL carries the poise of a seasoned leader and the soul of a storyteller. Grounded, soulful, precise, quietly courageous, and purposefully warm — these five traits shape every interaction. The brand doesn't shout, oversell, or perform. It shows up fully, speaks with purpose, and always leaves room for others. Like wind, it adapts and flows through resistance with calm, grounded articulation rather than force.

Brand as a Product

PCL delivers freely accessible, evidence-first research reports and leadership insights that bridge the gap between abstract academic research and commercial consultancy. Its unique lens explores not just what people value, but how similar or different they believe others' values to be. Extended offerings include workshops, advisory sessions, sector-specific materials, and youth outreach tools.

Brand as an Organisation

PCL is independent and self-funded, partnering only with organisations whose values align with its own. It operates across sectors and generations, proactively seeking new audiences rather than waiting for them to arrive. Founded on the conviction that research insights should be freely available, it serves as a megaphone for people's voices — never the judge of their values.
